Hydrogen

Hydrogen is a fuel that forms only water when used in a fuel cell. Hydrogen can be produced with solar and wind energy (renewable energy sources), nuclear energy, biomass, etc. These features make hydrogen a viable fuel option for vehicles and power generation.

It can be used in cars, houses and other fields. Hydrogen can be used to store and transport energy obtained in other ways.

Hydrogen can be obtained from natural gas and water electrolysis.

Technical Specifications

  • Energy density: 120-142 MJ/kg
  • Storage: Compressed gas (350-700 bar) or liquid (-253°C)
  • Production methods: Steam reforming, electrolysis, biomass gasification
  • Applications: Fuel cells, combustion engines, power generation

Biofuels

Biofuels are renewable fuels derived from organic matter, such as plants and algae. They can be used as alternatives to fossil fuels in transportation and power generation.

Technical Specifications

  • Types: Ethanol, biodiesel, biogas, bio-oil
  • Energy density: 20-40 MJ/kg (varies by type)
  • Production methods: Fermentation, transesterification, gasification
  • Applications: Transportation fuel, heating, electricity generation

Solar Energy

Solar energy harnesses the power of the sun through photovoltaic cells or solar thermal collectors. It's one of the cleanest and most abundant renewable energy sources available.

Technical Specifications

  • Efficiency: 15-22% for commercial PV panels
  • Types: Photovoltaic, concentrated solar power, solar thermal
  • Capacity factors: 10-25% (varies by location and technology)
  • Applications: Electricity generation, water heating, industrial processes

Wind Energy

Wind energy converts the kinetic energy of wind into electricity using wind turbines. It's one of the fastest-growing renewable energy sources worldwide.

Technical Specifications

  • Turbine capacity: 2-8 MW for onshore, up to 12 MW for offshore
  • Efficiency: 35-45% (Betz limit is 59.3%)
  • Capacity factors: 25-50% (varies by location)
  • Applications: Electricity generation, water pumping, hybrid systems
